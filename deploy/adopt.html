What to expect when living with a pig

  • Size of your pig

    Fully-grown pigs weigh between 100 and 150 pounds. Unlike the “teacup pig” myth, the smallest healthy size of a fill-grown pig is around 60 lb.

  • Other housemates

    If you live with small children or other pets like dogs, a pig may not feel safe and happy in your home.

  • Zoned for approval

    In the eyes of the law, any size pig is seen as livestock. Make sure your area is zoned before bringing home your pig.

  • Finding a vet

    Specific vets are needed to care for your pig, but we’ll help you out.

Adopt your new oinking friend

When you adopt a pig from one of our sanctuaries, we keep adoption fees low and make sure that your pig is healthy, well-socialized, and up-to-date on vaccines.

    If you adopt a full-grown potbellied pig, you'll know exactly what size pig you are getting.

  • Vaccines

    We keep track of all vaccines that every pig is administered and make sure they are up to date before adoption.
